What is Computational Thinking?

Computational thinking is a problem-solving approach used in Computer Science and programming alike. It involves breaking down problems, understandinding potential patterns and constructions step by step solutions. Concepts such as iteration, data structures (including graphs and trees) and recursion are key to this.

How is the course unit formatted?

This course is formatted through 6 blocks. Each block involves a task that we will have to complete as a group, and typically covers a key computing technique. Each block is assessed on the algorithm that is created, and its description and justification.

Is there anything else to be assessed on?

Yes, there is a exam which contributes to 50% of the grade, along with a feedback task that makes up 10%.
