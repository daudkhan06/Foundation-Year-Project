Daud Khan's Reflection

How has it improved my computational thinking skills?

The foundation year computational thinking module has broadened my understanding of what it means to think algorithmically. Rather than just focusing on writing code or learning a programming language, which is what I had experienced in sixth form, my understanding of how problems can be solved and algorithm analysis improved significantly.

My favourite part of computational thinking was route-finding using graphs to represent maps. Using graphs allowed finding all possible ways to reach one side of a map to another easier to visualise and saves a lot of time.

Hector Pearson's Reflection

How has it improved my computational thinking skills?

The foundation year computational thinking module has not only developed my skills as a computer scientist but also drawn away from the code heavy focus of A-Level and more towards the theories that form their foundation.

For example, I while I had previously gained knowledge surround Big O-Notation I had never run through an algorithm step-by-step to exactly understand where this notation came from and how it worked.

My favourite part of this module was looking at recursion and understanding its inner workings. In particular I enjoyed the group workshop around this subject as whiile I found it tricky, it in turn was very rewarding for me.

Albert Bradshaw's Reflection

How has it improved my computational thinking skills?

The foundation year computational thinking module has developed my ideas around computer science as whole alongside developing my skills in working as a team. I have learnt how to better collaborate with my coursemates and deliver my ideas in a more concise way.

In particular, I believe I gained skills on how to read algorithms and understand their flow to a higher degree. I think this was particularly developed by the workshop sessions where I challenged my groups ideas and learnt new information.

My favourite part of the computational thinking module was learning about the logic surrounding path-finding algorithms and their logical implications. I have an interest in videogames and so it was interesting to learn about this at the lowest level.
